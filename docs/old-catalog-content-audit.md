# 2023 CarbonZD Catalog Content Audit

Source reviewed: `2023CarbonZD.pdf`  
Source length: 8 pages  
Purpose of this audit: retain useful company and product information while rebuilding the catalog for international B2B buyers.

## Executive assessment

The 2023 catalog establishes the company name, brand direction, factory location, core manufacturing equipment and an initial product range. It also contains useful factory and product photography. However, it reads more like a short sales leaflet than a complete export catalog. Product families are mixed together, technical data is inconsistent, English requires substantial rewriting, and there is no structured path from product selection to quotation.

The new catalog should treat all numerical claims from the old catalog as company-supplied legacy information that must be reconfirmed before final publication. This especially applies to equipment count, annual capacity and mechanical-property values.

## Missing technical tables

The new catalog should use concise, quotation-oriented tables rather than unqualified performance claims.

- Woven fabric: product code, tow size, areal weight, weave, width and roll length.
- UD fabric: product code, areal weight, fiber/tow, width, roll length and intended process.
- Multiaxial NCF: architecture, fiber angles, total weight, width and stitching.
- Spread tow: tow format, fabric construction, areal weight, width and intended visual/structural use.
- Prepreg: reinforcement, fiber areal weight, resin system, resin content, backing, storage and cure profile.
- Pultruded plates: thickness, width, length, surface condition and verified mechanical data.
- Laminate sheets: thickness, sheet size, layup, finish and machining options.
- Tubes: process, OD, ID, wall thickness, length, surface and tolerance.
- Yarn/roving: tow size, grade, sizing, package and downstream process.
- Chopped fiber: cut length, sizing, moisture, package and matrix compatibility.
- Aramid/hybrid fabric: fiber combination, weight, weave, width and color.
- Accessories: material, dimensions/weight, compatibility and packing.

All mechanical values should identify whether they describe raw fiber, dry reinforcement, cured laminate or finished profile. Test method and typical-versus-guaranteed status must be explicit.

## Missing application sections

- Automotive and mobility components.
- Civil UAV, agricultural drone, mapping and inspection equipment.
- Sports equipment and leisure products.
- Marine structures and water-sports products.
- Construction reinforcement and infrastructure repair.
- Industrial automation, robotics and lightweight machine components.
- Composite tooling, plates, housings and custom CNC parts.

Application pages should explain selection logic, not imply certification or suitability without engineering review.

## Missing inquiry and quotation information

The old catalog gives contact details but does not tell buyers what to send. The new RFQ section should request:

- Product family and preferred product code.
- Fiber grade or tow size.
- Areal weight, weave or fiber orientation.
- Width, thickness, OD/ID, wall thickness or cut length as applicable.
- Roll length, sheet size or finished-part drawing.
- Resin system, sizing or surface finish.
- Required quantity and repeat demand.
- Application and manufacturing process.
- Delivery destination and preferred Incoterm.
- Required documents, inspection items and packaging.
- Target date and sample requirement.

The quotation page should also state that final specifications, tolerances, MOQ, lead time and documentation are confirmed in the commercial offer.

## Suggested improvements

1. Expand from 8 pages to a 32-page A4 catalog with clear section dividers.
2. Lead with product range and applications rather than a generic building image.
3. Separate product families that were previously combined.
4. Use short professional English, specification tables and quotation prompts.
5. Add a visible distinction between standard supply and custom-by-review options.
6. Remove or qualify mechanical values that do not identify material basis or test method.
7. Add quality-control, traceability, export packing and customization pages.
8. Use large replaceable image areas with consistent aspect ratios and captions.
9. Use one product-code system across the catalog and website.
10. Add a clear RFQ checklist and a single confirmed sales contact.
11. Keep certificates, test reports and customer logos out unless authentic files are supplied.
12. Add a final pre-publication verification pass for capacity, equipment count, address, contact details, product availability and all technical values.
